How this is calculated

Principal & interest use the standard amortizing-loan formula. For Monthly compounding the periodic rate is the annual rate ÷ 12. For Canadian semi-annual compounding (how fixed-rate Canadian mortgages legally work), the rate is compounded twice a year and converted to a monthly equivalent — so the same posted rate gives a slightly lower payment than a naïve US-style calculation. Taxes, insurance, HOA and PMI are added on top of principal & interest. PMI/CMHC is applied while the loan-to-value ratio is above 80% and removed automatically once you cross that line.

Estimate only. This is for planning, not a loan offer. It doesn't include closing costs, rate changes on variable mortgages, or lender-specific fees. Confirm exact figures with your lender. NiftyTools Hub is not a financial advisor.
